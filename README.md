# Adaptive-Adversarial-Scenario-Generation-Based-On-TimeGAN
## ABSTRACT 
Trustworthy and robust testing and evaluation are crucial for the safe and efficient deployment of connected and autonomous vehicles (CAVs). However, current field and road testing remains largely constrained to routine traffic conditions, making safety-critical events exceedingly rare, which arises a challenge commonly known as the “curse of rarity”. While recent learning-based scenario generation methods have expanded coverage of high-risk events, they typically produce generic test cases and overlook the distinct vulnerabilities of different controllers. Moreover, most of these approaches focus on high-conflict driving environments, such as merges and roundabouts. Longitudinal controllers, which are fundamental to CAV operation, are still predominantly tested merely using naturalistic driving data. To address this gap, a novel Adaptive Adversarial Scenario Generation via TimeGAN (AASG) is proposed to generate leading vehicle trajectories that strategically target controller-specific weaknesses in longitudinal driving. The proposed AAGS integrates an embedding-recovery autoencoder architecture, and it combines adversarial loss with stepwise supervisory loss, to ensure that generated trajectories adhere to the statistical and temporal characteristics of real traffic. Furthermore, a controller-specific evaluation loss, derived from the principles of CAV control theory, is introduced to quantitatively assess the influence of generated leading-vehicle trajectories on the control stability of following vehicles. With limited number of fine-tuning iterations, AASG can rapidly adapt to distinct controller types, producing realistic trajectories that not only emulate authentic traffic dynamics but also uncover unique vulnerabilities. Experimental results demonstrate that AASG can effectively expose critical deficiencies in controllers regarding safety, and stability, thereby enhancing the efficiency of CAV testing.
